  • Do not use metallic, wire-reinforced or conductive ladders or tools around live electrical equipment

08
HRCW — Fixing Edge Protection & Working at Height on Ladders, Trestles & Scaffold HRCW
Fall from height / ladder failure / falling objects / impact / fatality
IR: 4A
  • Perform as much of the task as possible on the ground or a solid structure; protect all edges where a person can fall
  • Erect and inspect all height access equipment following manufacturer's instructions; if damaged or unsuitable — take out of service immediately and apply LOTO procedures
  • Workers must be able to pass through edge protection without climbing over top or mid-rail
  • Trestle scaffold: Guardrails, toeboards and side boards in place and ends pinned; planks designed for purpose and minimum 450mm wide; plank thickness: 38mm = 1.5m span, 50mm = 2m span, 63mm = 2.5m span
  • Mobile scaffold: Level and plumb; casters locked before accessing, not moved when people are on it; accessed by internal ladder only — do not climb frame; clear of penetrations or floor edges by at least 1m; do not use steps or ladders on top of platform to gain extra height
  • Fixed scaffold: Undertake a handover inspection for all scaffolds where a person could fall more than 4 metres — scaffold not used until sighted proof it has been inspected and signed off by a competent person after construction, after repairs, and after any incident that may affect stability
  • Only competent scaffolder to make changes to scaffold; do not remove scaffold ties, handrails or boards; maintain awareness of other people on scaffold
  • Do not use step ladders to gain extra height; remove ground level ladder when unattended to prevent unauthorised access
  • Guards kept in position on scaffold lifts; waste materials removed from scaffolding and placed in waste receptacle
  • Do not exceed Safe Working Load of scaffold; stack items to prevent sliding or toppling; keep tools away from edges
  • Never work directly below other workers or where equipment is being placed above
  • Kickboards installed on elevated work areas to prevent items falling off
  • Ladders: Set up on firm level footing; base at 1/4 vertical height from wall; extends at least 1m above landing; secured at base and top against movement; 3 points of contact at all times; load rated minimum 120kg; correct size and length for job; no damage, clean and dry; 1 person per ladder
  • ONLY use extension ladders for access; ALWAYS use platform ladders for working on — base fully open, spreaders locked
  • Always face the ladder; do not carry materials when ascending/descending; do not over-reach — descend and reposition
  • Maintain majority of bodyweight inside perimeter of platform or stiles at all times
  • Falls in openings: All holes and openings protected/closed from falls; covers secured to prevent movement; use signage or hazard marking to identify the hazard
  • Wear tool belts; use tool lanyards to pass tools to people at height; tethers on tools/equipment where appropriate
  • All workers must wear a hard hat and high-visibility clothing
  • Footwear: snug-fitting shoes/boots with flat, non-slip soles — no loose soles, long laces, oily soles or mud-caked soles
RR: 2M
Electrocution
IR: 4A
  • Locate and maintain awareness of any power lines and extension leads when handling components
  • When erecting mobile scaffold: check for overhead wires or other electrical installations before erecting; check height, arrangement and visibility of overhead power lines
  • Do not use extended poles or other conductive items within 4m of energised electrical systems (flashover or contact may occur)
  • If the 4.0m clearance rule cannot be maintained — contact the State Electricity Supply Authority to determine a safe system of work before proceeding
  • Do not use metallic, wire-reinforced or conductive ladders around live electrical equipment
  • Follow Lock-out/Tag-out (LOTO) procedures; obtain advice from licensed electrician confirming power is isolated before work commences
RR: 2M
Falling objects
IR: 3H
  • Carry tools and materials in a tool belt; have them passed up to height using tool lanyards — do not throw
  • Determine where items may fall in case of uncontrolled release; ensure all personnel are clear of that area
RR: 2M

HRCW — Install Sheeting HRCW
Fall / suspension trauma / fatality
IR: 4A
  • Walk on intersections of battens and rafters only; walk on screw lines on tin roofs
  • Distribute sheeting evenly; limit sheets per stack; do not travel unnecessarily
  • Overlap sheets away from prevailing winds
  • Do not work close to, or with back to, roof edge within 2m
  • Never lunge for dropped objects; do not throw tools/materials from roof
  • Harness: inspect before use, correct size, within weight limits, no loose clothing, plan fall arrest system to eliminate pendulum/swing-back effect
  • Anchor points must comply with relevant AS/NZS standards
  • Suspension trauma rescue plan must be documented and rehearsed; rescue must commence within 5 minutes of a fall
RR: 2M
Falling objects
IR: 3H
  • Groundworkers wear hard hats; carry tools in tool belt
  • Lower all materials in a controlled motion — never throw
RR: 2M
Cuts / lacerations
IR: 3H
  • Use utility knives/metal snips correctly; cut away from body; do not catch falling tools
  • Wear appropriate gloves when handling sheet steel (sharp edges/burrs)
RR: 2M
Heat stress
IR: 3H
  • Recognise symptoms: mild (tired, cramps), severe (headache, rapid pulse, profuse sweating, confusion, vision issues)
  • Light protective clothing; extra rest breaks in cool area
  • Drink ~200mL water every 15–20 minutes in hot conditions
RR: 2M
✓
Supervisors confirm sheeting controls are in place and understood by all workers

Working at Height — Ladders, Trestles, Scaffold & Openings HRCW
Fall from height / falling objects / impact / fatality
IR: 4A
  • GENERAL: Perform as much work as possible on the ground or solid structure; protect all edges where a person can fall
  • Erect and inspect all height access equipment following manufacturer's instructions; take out of service immediately if damaged — apply LOTO
  • TRESTLE SCAFFOLD: Guardrails, toeboard, side boards in place; planks designed for purpose; minimum 450mm wide; plank thickness: 38mm = 1.5m span, 50mm = 2m span, 63mm = 2.5m span
  • MOBILE SCAFFOLD: Level and plumb; casters locked before accessing; never moved when people are on it; accessed by internal ladder only — do not climb scaffold frame; clear of penetrations/floor edges by at least 1m
  • FIXED SCAFFOLD: Handover inspection by competent person before use; re-inspected after construction, after repairs, after any incident that may affect stability
  • Only competent scaffolder to make changes; do not remove handrails or boards; maintain awareness of other people on scaffold
  • Do not exceed specified Safe Working Load; do not use step ladders for extra height; remove ground level ladder when unattended
  • Waste materials removed from scaffolding and placed in waste receptacle
  • LADDERS: Set up on firm level footing; base at 1/4 vertical height from wall; extends 1m above landing; secured at base and top; 3 points of contact at all times; load rated minimum 120kg; face ladder when ascending/descending
  • ONLY use extension ladders for access; ALWAYS use platform ladders for working on
  • Do not carry materials when ascending/descending; do not over-reach; descend and reposition
  • FALLS IN OPENINGS: All holes/openings protected/closed; covers secured to prevent movement; use signage to identify hazard
  • Wear tool belts; use tool lanyards to pass tools to height; tethers on equipment
  • All workers: hard hat and high-vis; footwear snug-fitting, flat non-slip soles

  • Follow Lock-out/Tag-out (LOTO) procedures and obtain advice from licensed electrician that power is isolated

▼

5
Hierarchy of Controls
▼
1st
Elimination — remove the hazard completely
2nd
Substitution — replace with something less hazardous
3rd
Isolation — separate people from the hazard
4th
Engineering controls — e.g. edge protection, scaffolding, EWP
5th
Administrative controls — e.g. training, SWMS, signage, supervision
6th
PPE — last resort; does not eliminate the hazard
Always use the highest practicable level of control. Multiple controls may be combined. PPE should never be the sole control measure for HRCW.
